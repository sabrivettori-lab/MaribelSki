Five days exploring all 3 valleys from Méribel Centre (La Chaudanne). Each day covers different terrain. For slope lunches and evening restaurants, browse the restaurant guide — grouped by area so you can pick based on where you're skiing.

Day 1 — Méribel Home Valley

Méribel Valley
Sunday 22 March
9:00 AM — Morning
Altiport warm-up
La Chaudanne → RHODOS gondola → ski Blanchot and Rhodos around the Altiport area (gentle, wide). Then try Lapin — fun hairpin bends through the forest.
11:00 AM — Late morning
Saulire mid-station circuit
Back to La Chaudanne → SAULIRE EXPRESS 1 to mid-station → ski Biche then Marmotte back to La Chaudanne. The classic Méribel blue circuit.
2:30 PM — Afternoon
Toward Mottaret & home
LEGENDS chairlift → Gélinotte toward Mottaret → Truite through the trees → Doron home to La Chaudanne by 4pm.
Learn the Doron run today — it's your highway home from almost anywhere in Méribel.

Day 2 — Méribel → Courchevel

Courchevel Valley
Monday 23 March
9:00 AM — Morning
Over the Saulire to Courchevel
La Chaudanne → SAULIRE EXPRESS 1 → SAULIRE EXPRESS 2 to summit (2,740m — photo stop!) → drop into Courchevel via Creux. One of the best blues in the 3 Valleys.
10:30 AM — Mid-morning
Courchevel circuit & Col de la Loze
From Courchevel 1850 → CHENUS gondola → Gentianes → confident skiers add Marquis → DOU DES LANCHES → up to Col de la Loze panoramic viewpoint.
2:30 PM — Afternoon
La Tania forest & home
From Col de la Loze → Folyères through enormous spruce trees to La Tania (magical tree run) → LA TANIA gondola → back over SAULIRE EXPRESS 2 → Biche → Doron home. Be back by 4pm.
If it's snowing, skip Saulire summit and head straight to La Tania forest — Folyères is sheltered and beautiful in any weather.

Day 3 — Méribel → Val Thorens

Val Thorens · 3,200m
Tuesday 24 March
9:00 AM — Morning
Cross to Val Thorens via Col de la Chambre
La Chaudanne → LEGENDS to Mottaret → PLATTIERES gondola → CÔTE BRUNE (new 10-seat gondola) to Col de la Chambre (2,850m) → ski Pluviomètre into Val Thorens.
10:30 AM — High altitude
Cime de Caron — 3,200m summit
VT13 FUNITEL PÉCLET → Moraine → VT33 CIME CARON cable car to 3,200m. Mont Blanc, Écrins, even the Matterhorn on a clear day. Confident skiers: Combe de Caron down. Others: ride the cable car back.
2:30 PM — Return
Back to Méribel
VT2 PIONNIERS → VT4 3 VALLÉES back to Col de la Chambre → Lac de la Chambre (long blue to Mottaret) → Doron home. Leave Val Thorens by 3pm latest.
Even if you don't ski the black from Cime de Caron, ride the cable car for the 360° view. Absolutely breathtaking.

Day 4 — Belleville Valley

Saint-Martin · Les Menuires
Wednesday 25 March
9:00 AM — Morning
Over Tougnète to Belleville
La Chaudanne → LEGENDS → Grive → TOUGNÈTE 1 → TOUGNÈTE 2 to summit (2,437m) → drop into Belleville via Roc de Fer (long, sunny, stunning views) down to Saint-Martin village.
10:30 AM — Mid-morning
Pointe de la Masse — 1,000m vertical blue
From Saint-Martin → ST MARTIN EXPRESS → ski to Les Menuires → POINTE DE LA MASSE lifts to 2,804m → ski Bouquetin. 1,000m vertical descent — one of the best blues in the 3 Valleys.
2:30 PM — Return
Secret Raffort forest run home
From Les Menuires → ROC DE TOUGNE → back via Tougnète ridge → ski Raffort — quiet forest blue, almost nobody on it → Méribel Village → Doron home.
Raffort is the hidden gem of Méribel — quiet forest, beautiful light, barely anyone else on it. Don't skip it.

Day 5 — Grand Tour (all 3 Valleys)

All 3 Valleys in one day
Thursday 26 March
9:00 AM — Courchevel
Fast hit over Saulire
First lift → SAULIRE EXPRESS 1 & 2 to summit → Creux into Courchevel (freshly groomed, glorious) → quick lap: VIZELLE → back to 1850 → SAULIRE back to Méribel side.
10:30 AM — Val Thorens
Cross to the highest resort
Ski to Mottaret → PLATTIERES → CÔTE BRUNE to Col de la Chambre → Pluviomètre into Val Thorens → fast laps: VT2 PIONNIERS → Cairn → back up VT4 3 VALLÉES.
2:30 PM — Belleville & home
Touch the third valley
From Col de la Chambre → Lac de la Chambre to Mottaret → COMBES 2 → TOUGNÈTE 2 → quick run via Gros Tougne toward Les Menuires → back via ROC DE TOUGNE → Alouette → Doron home by 3:30pm.
This day is about covering ground, not lapping runs. Keep moving — you'll cross 3 valleys and 600km of connected terrain in one day.

Key Lifts to Memorise

SAULIRE EXPRESS 1 & 2 = Méribel ↔ Courchevel
PLATTIERES + CÔTE BRUNE = Mottaret → Val Thorens (via Col de la Chambre)
VT2 PIONNIERS + VT4/VT5 3 VALLÉES = Val Thorens → Méribel (back to Col de la Chambre)
TOUGNÈTE 1 & 2 = Méribel ↔ Belleville Valley
LEGENDS = La Chaudanne → Mottaret direction
RHODOS = La Chaudanne → Altiport (warm-up/beginners)

Doron = your highway home to La Chaudanne from almost anywhere in Méribel
Bad Weather Plan

Low visibility? Stay in the trees:

Folyères — La Tania forest, sheltered, beautiful even in a blizzard
Lapin & Rhodos — Altiport area, forest runs, lower altitude
Raffort — Tougnète (Méribel side), quiet forest

Avoid anything above treeline: Val Thorens glacier, Cime de Caron, Saulire summit
