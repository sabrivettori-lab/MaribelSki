The Fancy Nights Out

For when you want to dress up (as much as ski boots allow) and treat yourselves.

La Bouitte

3 Michelin Stars Must-Do
Saint-Martin-de-Belleville (slopes access) $$$$ ★★★★½ 4.5 TripAdvisor (631 reviews) · 4.8 Google
+33 4 79 08 96 77
Website | Google Maps
The undisputed crown jewel of the 3 Vallées. Father-son duo René and Maxime Meilleur run a family restaurant that happens to hold 3 Michelin stars. Local Savoyard ingredients elevated to extraordinary heights — Arctic char from Lake Geneva, ancient vegetables, unpasteurised cheeses. The 3-course lunch menu starts around €210pp; the full tasting runs to €440pp. For 7 people, expect around €3,500–5,000+ with wine. They provide a shuttle from the slopes.
"The best 3-star Michelin I have ever been to: it is casual, not over-expensive, and you can almost ski to it." — TripAdvisor
"We experienced genuine culinary mastery at work. The breads were freshly baked, tasty with wonderful variety." — TripAdvisor

L'Ekrin by Laurent Azoulay

1 Michelin Star Top Pick
Méribel Centre (Hôtel Le Kaïla) $$$$ ★★★★½ 4.6 TripAdvisor (125 reviews) · 4.2 Google
+33 4 79 41 69 35
Website | Google Maps
The only Michelin-starred restaurant in Méribel. Chef Laurent Azoulay blends Provence and Savoie influences in a cosy, luxurious chalet setting with bespoke ceramics. The tasting menu runs from €95 for 3 courses up to the full experience with wine pairings. A vegetarian tasting menu is also offered. For 7 people, budget around €1,500–3,000+ depending on wine choices. Easier to get a reservation than La Bouitte.
"There was a moment of blissful silence with the first bite of each dish. Well deserved Michelin star, better than many one-stars I've been to." — TripAdvisor

La Coursive des Alpes

Top Pick
Méribel Centre $$$ ★★★★½ Highly regarded locally
+33 4 79 06 44 97
Website | Google Maps
Chef Laurent Hillairet trained in Parisian palaces and brings creative, seasonal cuisine to a modern and chic setting. A strong alternative to L'Ekrin for an elevated dinner without the Michelin premium. Quality seasonal ingredients prepared with Parisian-palace precision in a more relaxed setting.

Authentic & Unmissable Dinners

The restaurants where the food genuinely shines, the setting is memorable, and the experience feels real.

Chez Pépé Nicolas

Must-Do Unique Experience Great Value
Between Les Ménuires & Val Thorens (Piste de la Chasse) $$ ★★★★★ 4.6 TripAdvisor (1,252 reviews) · 4.8 Google · 4.9 Trip.com
+33 6 09 45 28 35
Website | Google Maps
This is the one. A family-run farmhouse restaurant on a mountainside where the Suchet family has been farming for generations. They raise their own animals, grow produce in a high-altitude permaculture garden, and make their own cheeses. The setting is spectacular — animals roaming outside, stunning views, rustic-chic decor. Savoyard classics done perfectly. Around €50–70pp with wine. Free shuttle from the slopes. For 7 people this is an absolute no-brainer.
"This is by far and away the best restaurant in the 3 Valleys. Phenomenal local cuisine, friendly staff, stunning view." — Google
"The farm with goats being milked was a special experience. The food and service were perfect." — Trip.com

La Fromagerie

Great Value Top Pick
Méribel Centre $$ ★★★★ 4.1 TripAdvisor (196 reviews) · 4.6 Google
+33 4 79 08 55 48
Website | Google Maps
Enter through a cheese shop, descend into a vaulted basement, and prepare for the best fondue and raclette in Méribel. This is the place for a loud, fun, cheesy (literally) group night. You'll walk out smelling of cheese and you won't care. Book ahead especially on Wednesdays when chalet staff are off. For 7 people sharing fondues, raclettes, charcuterie and wine, expect around €40–50pp.
"Possibly the best fondue in France. The aroma of cheese hits you as soon as you open the door!" — TripAdvisor

Restaurant du Grand Coeur

Méribel Centre $$$ ★★★★ Well-regarded locally
+33 4 79 08 60 03
Website | Google Maps
Refined gourmet cuisine in a beautiful traditional Savoyard setting. Excellent wine list and good cocktails at the bar. A reliable choice for an elevated dinner without the Michelin-star formality — think upscale comfort food with a gorgeous setting.

La Vache Rouge

Top Pick
Méribel Centre (formerly Le Cèpe) $$$ ★★★★ Strong reputation (reviews from Le Cèpe era)
Freshly rebranded for the 2025/26 season (previously the well-loved Le Cèpe), La Vache Rouge offers exceptional meats and seafood in a chic, contemporary Alpine setting. The previous incarnation was praised for its porcini mushroom cappuccino soup, foie gras, and quality wine list. Now focused on prime character meats and premium seafood. Open daily. A great way to kick off the week — upscale enough to feel like an arrival celebration, without the Michelin formality. Expect around €80–100pp with wine.
"The porcini cappuccino is a must-try. Amazing wine list. From foie gras through to the desserts, this was a terrific meal." — Wanderlog (Le Cèpe era)
Group Booking Tip
For 7 people, book at least a few days ahead at all restaurants below. For La Bouitte, book 4–6 weeks minimum. For Chez Pépé Nicolas, use their free shuttle from the slopes or arrange the evening shuttle for dinner.

Méribel Slope Lunches

Your home valley — all accessible from La Chaudanne without crossing a ridge.

L'Adray Telebar Méribel

Must-Do Great Value
Méribel (Piste du Doron, foot of Adret chair) $$ ★★★★½ Beloved institution · Foursquare 8.4/10
+33 4 79 08 60 26
Website | Google Maps
The legendary escalope à la crème that everyone raves about. A family-run chalet hotel on the slopes with a sunny south-facing terrace and valley views. The 3-course daily lunch menu (€35–40) is outstanding value. Second-generation cooks using only fresh, local products. Arrive early or book ahead — ski instructors eat here on their day off, which tells you everything. One of the oldest restaurants in the valley.
"Having eaten at almost every restaurant in Méribel/Mottaret/Courchevel, I rate this as up there with the best." — TripAdvisor
