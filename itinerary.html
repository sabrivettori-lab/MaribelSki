Groceries & Wine

Where to stop on the way

Best stop: Intermarche Hyper, Albertville (Zone Cassine) — the last full-size supermarket before you head up the mountain. Huge selection, good wine aisle, local Savoyard products (Beaufort, crozets, diots sausages, charcuterie), and proper prices. Much cheaper than anything in-resort. If your shuttle allows a stop, this is the one. Open Mon-Sat 8:30am-7:15pm.

Also in Albertville: Intermarche Super (Rue Pasteur) and Carrefour Market — both solid alternatives if Hyper is out of the way.

For wine: Intermarche Hyper has a decent selection, but if you want something special, look for a caviste (wine shop) in Albertville. Grab Savoyard whites (Apremont, Chignin-Bergeron) and reds (Mondeuse) — they pair perfectly with mountain food and are great value. For splurge bottles, buy in the valley. Resort shops charge a premium.

In resort (top-ups): SPAR Meribel (Mussillon, largest in-resort), U Express (Rond-Point), Sherpa (Mottaret and Meribel 1600), Casino Shop (centre). All carry basics but at resort prices — expect 30-50% more than Albertville. Sherpa stores loan raclette and fondue equipment for free.

For cheese: La Fromagerie has an upstairs cheese shop selling excellent local cheeses during the day. The Cooperative Laitiere de Moutiers (on the road up) sells mature Beaufort directly.

* At L'Adray Telebar, the €35-40 set lunch is exceptional value for slope dining

* Skip wine pairings at Michelin restaurants and order by the bottle — better value for a group
